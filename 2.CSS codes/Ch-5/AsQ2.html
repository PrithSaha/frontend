A paragraph is a distinct unit of writing, typically composed of multiple sentences, that focuses on a single idea or topic. It serves to organize and structure longer pieces of writing, making them easier to follow. Paragraphs are usually set off by indentation at the beginning of the first sentence. Here's a more detailed breakdown: Single Topic Focus: Each paragraph should center around one central idea or topic. Structure: A paragraph typically includes a topic sentence (which introduces the main idea), supporting sentences that develop the topic, and often a concluding sentence. Indentation: In most writing styles, a paragraph begins with an indentation on the first line. Organization: Paragraphs help to break down complex information into smaller, more manageable chunks, improving readability. Length: While there's no strict rule, paragraphs are generally several sentences long. Academic paragraphs are often between 200 and 300 words, according to the University of Hull. Purpose: Paragraphs can be used to explain, describe, narrate, compare, or persuade, depending on the overall purpose of the writing.
